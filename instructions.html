Welcome to Texas Holdem!

Game Objective

Try to collect all your opponents chips by winning a round (more on that later)

Game Setup

This game is played with a standard 52 card deck

Each Player is dealt two cards, with four rounds of betting and a showdown at the end to determine who wins the pot

Turn Setup:

Small and Big Blinds:

Before each hand, two players are designated as the "small blind" and "big blind". The small blind is required to make a small bet, and the big blind makes double the bet.

Player Order

The player to the left(clockwise) of the big blind starts the betting. Betting proceeds clockwise around the table.

How does betting work?

Actions:

Players can take various actions during their turn.

"Checking" is when you bet nothing.

"Raising" is when you want to bet more than the previous person.

"Calling" is when you match the previous persons bet.

"Folding" is when you decide not to match the bet and give up on winning the pot.

How does betting work? Pt2

Pre-Flop:

After receiving their hole cards, the player to the left of the big blind starts the betting. Players have the option to bet, raise, or fold.

Flop:

Three community cards are dealt face-up on the table.A second round of betting ensues starting with the small blind.

Turn:

A fourth community card, known as the "turn", is revealed. Another round of betting resumes, with the small blind.

River:

The fifth and final community card, known as the "river", is revealed. One last round of betting takes place.

How to win a round:

Everyone else folds

If all other players fold their hands, the last remaining player wins the pot. This can be done through bluffing or a lack of a good hand from the other player.

Winning showdown

In a showdown, the player with the best hand (highest-ranking combination) wins the pot. This occurs after the river betting is done. The hand of five is created

Showdown: Hand Rankings

Straight Flush:

A Straight Flush consists of five consecutive cards of the same suit. For example: 7♠ 8♠ 9♠ 10♠ J♠.

Four of a Kind:

Four cards of the same rank, along with any fifth card. For example: 8♠ 8♦ 8♣ 8♥ 2♠.

Full House:

A combination of three of a kind and a pair. For example: 5♠ 5♦ 5♣ 7♠ 7♦.

Showdown: Hand Rankings Pt2

Flush:

A Flush contains five cards of the same suit, not in sequence. For example: 2♥ 5♥ 7♥ 9♥ K♥.

Straight:

A Straight is a sequence of five cards in consecutive order, not all of the same suit. For example: 6♠ 7♦ 8♥ 9♠ 10♣.

Three of a Kind:

Three cards of the same rank, with two unrelated side cards. For example: 9♠ 9♦ 9♣ 3♠ 7♥.

Showdown: Hand Rankings Pt3

Two Pair:

Two Pair consists of two sets of pairs, along with an unrelated fifth card. For example: 8♠ 8♦ 4♣ 4♠ Q♠.

One Pair:

One Pair contains two cards of the same rank, along with three unrelated side cards. For example: J♠ J♦ 5♣ 9♠ 3♠.

High Card:

High Card is when none of the above combinations are achieved. The hand with the highest single card wins. For example: A♠ 10♦ 8♣ 6♠ 3♠.
